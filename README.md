# Procrastination Risk Detector

A simple Python program to check if you’re actually studying as much as you planned. Made this for my AI & ML assignment at VIT Bhopal.

---

## About the Project

So basically procrastination is something we all do, especially when we have a lot to study. You plan to study 4 hours, but then you end up scrolling Instagram or watching YouTube and somehow only study 1 hour. That’s basically what this program is about.

I made this little program to see if I’m actually following my study plan or just procrastinating. You just enter how many hours you planned to study, how many you actually did, and how many times you got distracted. Then it tells you if your risk is low, moderate, or high. It’s simple but kinda useful.

---

## Features

- Easy to use, no fancy stuff.
- Takes your planned study hours, actual hours, and distractions.
- Decides your procrastination risk.
- Shows a little report and gives a tip.
- Runs in the terminal (no need for any extra setup).

---

## How It Works

When you run the program, it asks:

1. How many hours you planned to study today  
2. How many hours you actually studied  
3. How many times you got distracted (like checking phone, watching TV, etc.)

Then it checks these conditions:

- If you studied what you planned and had less than 2 distractions → **Low Risk**  
- If you studied at least half of what you planned → **Moderate Risk**  
- If you studied less than half of your plan → **High Risk**

Finally it prints a small report and tells you your risk level.
